The press

Common Press publishes essays and short fiction by writers thinking about everyday life — the small, ordinary, often-overlooked moments that turn out to matter. A quarterly. Print-first. Designed to live on a bedside table for a season.

The brand is deliberately quiet. No tagline shouts. No badge winks. White space and type do the work. If Repair Club is a club you join, Common Press is a book you keep.

The angle

For the portfolio, this piece is the counterweight — a restrained, type-led visual world that proves the system thinking can hold something quiet too. Different palette, different typeface, different temperature.

What stays consistent: a strong italic accent on one word, generous breathing room, a confident hierarchy. The hand is the same; the brand world isn't.

Essay · 01 · The Inheritance

Notes on a bowl.

by Mira Augusto

My grandmother's serving bowl arrived in a box marked fragile · this end up, wrapped in an old t-shirt and three layers of bubble wrap that had been used and reused so many times the plastic had gone soft. The bowl itself is heavy ceramic, glazed in a green the colour of moss after rain, and chipped along the rim in three places. It is the kind of object you would not buy. It is the kind of object you might inherit.

I have it on the kitchen counter now. It holds, on any given day, oranges, mail, keys, a single broken AirPod waiting to be paired with its twin. It is doing none of the things it was made for, and somehow it has not stopped being a serving bowl.

Common Press · No. 01 · p. 04

What does it mean to receive a thing? Not just an object — a posture toward the world, a way of holding cutlery, a tendency to over-buy fruit for guests who are not yet coming. We inherit far more than we are willing to admit and most of it has nothing to do with money.

"It is the kind of object you would not buy. It is the kind of object you might inherit."

The bowl has a small hairline crack on the inside that you only see when it is empty. My grandmother filled it with so many things in her life — pasta, peeled potatoes, peppermints, once a kitten — that I can't think of the bowl without thinking of those things too. To inherit it is to inherit the things it once held, in some quiet way.

It is heavier than it looks. Almost everything inherited is.

Common Press · No. 01 · p. 05

What this proves.

Repair Club is loud, conspiratorial, and chromatic. Common Press is quiet, considered, and restrained. The point of building both is that the eye behind them can hold either world — and the design move that connects them (italic accent, sentence case, sentence-level care) is consistent across.

What I'd build next.

A fourth issue, an end-of-year compendium, a subscriber gift, a reading-series poster system. If the press ran for a year, the visual system can carry all of it.
