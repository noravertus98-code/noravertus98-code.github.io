Case study · 01

Repair Club.

A fictional community repair café where membership is free — the only rule is showing up with something broken.

Year

2026

Role

Direction · Design · Copy

Scope

Identity · print · web · interactive

Tools

Affinity Suite · HTML/JS

The brand

The name does the work. "Club" reframes repair as something you join, not something you do alone. The conspiratorial wink is built into the premise — you're in on this, with everyone else who's in on it. Membership is free. The only requirement is showing up with something broken.

Tagline: The only club where everyone wins.

The angle

I'm interested in the things people pass on — and the work it takes to make them feel chosen. Repair sits between I'm too cheap to replace this and this is mine and I'd like to keep it. The design world I built leans into the second meaning, hard.

A confident chromatic four — cobalt, red-orange, mustard, lavender pink — held by a calm cream and charcoal. No color is the brand color; the system flexes piece by piece, the way a real club's materials would.

Your diagnosis

Why this project.

I built Repair Club as a parallel project to a campaign I'm applying to design for an actual nonprofit. The applied work uses my real ideas; this one tests the system at the same scope without burning anything for the application.

The point of the exercise: show that I can hold a visual world across formats, not just make one good piece.

What I'd build next.

A short-run printed booklet — a member zine or repair primer with tips and stories. Member cards. Workshop signage system. If the brand ran for a year, the system can hold it.
