Notes · toward a campaign

Three early thoughts on the 2026 Reuse Awareness Campaign.

Not a pitch. Not the campaign. Just the first sketches — voice and direction before deliverables.

If I were starting tomorrow, here's the shape of the conversation I'd want to have. Each tile below picks at one facet of the social benefits the brief calls out — community, connection, intention — and tests a voice for it. The tone is consistent across, the angles are deliberately different. Three doors into the same room.

01 · The fix

From sacrifice to flex.

Reframe reuse as a thing you're good at, not a thing you settle for. Communities of people who reuse already exist — they trade tips, share sources, fix each other's lamps. The campaign invites the audience into a club they didn't realize was meeting.

02 · The fix

From solo to social.

The cure for loneliness isn't an app. It's knowing where the good thrift bins are and who to text when you find something for them. Reuse gives people a reason to know each other.

03 · The fix

From trend to taste.

Intentional is the actual flex now. The campaign positions reuse as the move of people who think for themselves — not nostalgia for the past, but agency in the present.

If we work together, none of this is the campaign — it's the warm-up.

The actual creative would come out of the kickoff conversation and the audience you have in mind for 2026. These sketches are here so you can see how I'd start: what I'd argue for, what I'd push back on, what kind of voice I'd want to set.

The full thinking is in my Repair Club case study — the brand world that's closest to the energy I'd bring here.
